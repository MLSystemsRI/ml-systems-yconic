- Computer vision + ML Material IDs (provenance system) track what gets loaded
- No cashier, no receipt — contractor drives in, picks materials from the bay, drives out, account charged
- Every material carries an ML ID with grade, contamination status, and full audit trail
- DEM compliance maintained automatically through the provenance chain
- Multi-level advantage: floors can specialize (Floor 1: high-turnover lumber/concrete, Floor 2: doors/windows/trim, Floors 3–4: bulk storage/staging)

The garage is the supply chain. The marketplace is a parking structure with a camera system and a database.

### Why Both Programs Need the Garage

The garage is not just storage — it's the **documented procurement channel** that government programs require. Every material carries an ML Material ID, grade, contamination status, and audit trail. When a grant pro forma shows "recovered lumber: $X," that price is documented in a live marketplace with provenance — not a guess.

**The demand flywheel:** EOH/NACA projects pull materials from BOH (guaranteed internal demand) → each decon adds materials to BOH (growing inventory) → surplus sold to external buyers (contractors, DIYers, Habitat) → community sees it happen → next buyer comes from the crowd → more decon demand → flywheel spins.

**Grant stacking:** EOH grant funds + NACA rate buydown. A NACA buyer using DPA funds can buy down their rate to potentially sub-2%. Combined with $0 down/no PMI + decon materials lowering TDC = genuinely affordable homeownership for 25–50% AMI families.
